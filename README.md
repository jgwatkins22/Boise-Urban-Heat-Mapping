# Project Title: Urban Heat Patterns in Boise, Idaho

Link: https://arcg.is/014eC41

## Overview
This project looks at a combination of urban heat patterns, vegetation cover, and socioeconomic factors in Boise, Idaho. By analyzing these variables, the study aims to reveal potential environmental inequalities and identify areas where strategic investment in green infrastructure could yield the greatest impact.
## Methodology
Data Sources: The analysis utilized Landsat 9 thermal imagery captured on July 11th, 2023, to visualize surface temperature variations across the city.

Spatial Analysis: The data was processed into a mosaic to evaluate surface temperature, tree canopy density, and median household income across census tracts.

Statistical Modeling: The study assessed correlations between variables, noting that while economic and environmental factors overlap, they influence heat patterns in distinct ways.
## Key Findings
Canopy Influence: Tree canopy coverage emerged as the most significant factor influencing Boise’s urban heat, confirming it as the city's most effective natural cooling mechanism ($R^2 = 0.36$).

Socioeconomic Context: The relationship between income and heat exposure ($R^2 = 0.0074$) is relatively weak compared to larger, older U.S. cities, suggesting that Boise's smaller size and resource distribution may currently buffer some environmental inequalities.

Planning Implications: Findings highlight that historic development and planning choices continue to influence canopy distribution, reinforcing the importance of prioritizing green space in future urban growth strategies.
